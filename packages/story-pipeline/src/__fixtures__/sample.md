# India's Rooftop Solar Surge

By the Energy Desk, March 2026

India added a record 18.7 gigawatts of rooftop solar capacity in the financial
year ending March 2026, a 33% jump over the previous year. The growth was led by
residential installations under the PM Surya Ghar subsidy scheme, which crossed
10 million homes for the first time.

Three states accounted for more than half of the new capacity. Gujarat led with
4.1 GW, followed by Maharashtra at 3.6 GW and Rajasthan at 2.9 GW. Officials
attribute the concentration to faster discom approvals and higher residential
tariffs, which shorten payback periods to under five years.

The cost of a typical 3 kW residential system fell to about 1.4 lakh rupees
before subsidy, down from 1.9 lakh two years earlier. Module prices, which make
up roughly 55% of system cost, drove most of the decline.

Analysts caution that grid integration remains the binding constraint. Several
state distribution companies have paused net-metering approvals in pockets where
feeder-level solar penetration exceeds 40%, citing afternoon voltage swings.
