## ITC's Resilient FY2026

ITC Limited delivered a robust financial performance for fiscal 2026, generating Rs 80,867 crore in standalone gross revenue—a 10.1 percent year-over-year increase. The conglomerate successfully navigated significant macroeconomic headwinds, including supply chain disruptions and deferred sales triggered by the ongoing West Asia conflict, to post a 4.9 percent rise in EBITDA to Rs 25,208 crore. For retail investors, this resilience translated into a total dividend declaration of Rs 14.50 per share, representing a substantial cash outflow of Rs 18,167 crore.

Top-line growth was structurally driven by double-digit expansion in the non-cigarette FMCG segments and a rapidly scaling digital-first and organic portfolio, which reached an annual revenue run rate of over Rs 1,350 crore. The strategic amalgamation of Sresta Natural Bioproducts brought the 24 Mantra Organic brand into the fold, alongside a massive network of 27,500 farmers across 1.4 lakh acres. Concurrently, ITC initiated staggered pricing interventions in its core cigarette business to offset unprecedented tax hikes and mitigate the escalating risk of illicit trade, which currently drains an estimated Rs 23,000 crore annually from the Exchequer.

## Top-Line Growth

ITC Limited closed FY2026 with standalone gross revenue of Rs 80,867.49 crore, a 10.1 percent increase over the prior year's Rs 73,466.61 crore — a result management characterised as resilient given the dual headwinds of an unprecedented cigarette tax hike and supply chain disruptions tied to the West Asia conflict.

EBITDA expanded 4.9 percent to Rs 25,208.22 crore, while Profit Before Exceptional Items and Tax came in at Rs 26,951.47 crore. Earnings Per Share from continuing operations rose to Rs 16.20 (FY25: Rs 16.07), underpinning a total dividend of Rs 14.50 per share — a combined interim and final payout translating to a cash outflow of Rs 18,167.57 crore.

The second half of FY2026 showed clear acceleration: H2 gross revenue and EBITDA grew 12.2 percent and 7.5 percent respectively, signalling that management's staggered pricing response to the February 2026 tax changes and its diversified portfolio strategy are gaining traction heading into FY2027.

## Structural Resilience

ITC's standalone gross revenue for FY2026 reached Rs 80,867.49 crore, a 10.1 percent increase year-on-year, demonstrating the company's capacity to grow through macro turbulence. EBITDA rose 4.9 percent to Rs 25,208.22 crore, while Profit Before Exceptional Items and Tax stood at Rs 26,951.47 crore — a meaningful expansion on a high base.

The second half of the year was particularly strong, with standalone gross revenue and EBITDA growing 12.2 percent and 7.5 percent respectively in H2 FY2026, indicating accelerating momentum as the year progressed. Earnings Per Share for the full year came in at Rs 16.20, marginally ahead of Rs 16.07 in FY2025.

Shareholder returns remained robust: the Board recommended a final dividend of Rs 8.00 per share, bringing total dividends for FY2026 to Rs 14.50 per share — up from Rs 14.35 in FY2025 — and implying a total cash outflow of Rs 18,167.57 crore. ## Segment Breakdown

ITC’s Rs 80,867 crore standalone gross revenue for FY2026 reflects a distinct duality in its structural growth engine. While the core FMCG-Cigarettes segment remains the undisputed heavyweight, posting Rs 11,066 crore in Q4 FY2026 standalone revenue, the non-cigarette FMCG portfolio is rapidly capturing ground.

The FMCG-Others segment delivered robust 15 percent year-over-year revenue growth in the final quarter. This top-line expansion is increasingly anchored by digital-first and organic portfolios—bolstered by the amalgamation of Sresta Natural Bioproducts and its 24 Mantra Organic brand—which surged 60 percent to achieve an annual revenue run-rate exceeding Rs 1,350 crore.
