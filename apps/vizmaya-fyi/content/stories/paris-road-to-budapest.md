# Paris: Road to Budapest

*One Champions League run, told end to end. The league-phase table that nearly sent them home, the four two-legged ties that didn't, and the bracket that carried Paris to Budapest — every panel hand-authored against the same data contract footshorts renders.*

**By vismay · footshorts**

---

## Eleventh, and through

The new league phase is unforgiving at the bottom. Paris won four, drew two, lost two — fourteen points, a goal difference of plus ten built on a wild twenty-one scored and eleven conceded. That was good for eleventh: above Newcastle only on goals scored, a single point behind Inter, and exactly on the line that separates the knockout play-offs from the plane home.

It was not control. It was survival. But survival was all they needed — eleventh meant a play-off, and a play-off meant the run could begin.

## Play-off: past Monaco

The reward for finishing eleventh was a tie against a domestic rival who knew them too well. Monaco took the first leg by the throat and led; Paris dug out a 3–2 win on the road anyway, the kind of away result that decides ties before they're over.

The return was tighter — 2–2, nervy, settled only on aggregate. 5–4 across the two legs, and Paris were into the Round of 16 with the narrowest margin of the whole run.

## Round of 16: Chelsea swept aside

Then the gears caught. Chelsea were taken apart 5–2 in Paris, a first leg so complete the second was a formality. It wasn't: Paris went to London and won 3–0 anyway.

8–2 on aggregate. The bottom seed of the league phase had just put eight goals past a Champions League knockout side across two legs, and the bracket suddenly looked very different.

## Quarter-final: Liverpool shut out

Against Liverpool the story flipped from goals to control. 2–0 at home, nothing given away; 2–0 again away, the tie killed without conceding a single goal across one hundred and eighty minutes.

4–0, two clean sheets, a quarter-final won on discipline rather than chaos. The team that scored twenty-one in the league phase had learned to win by not conceding.

## Semi-final: a night against Bayern

The semi-final was the opposite of the quarter — the best night of the run and almost the end of it. Paris and Bayern traded nine goals in the first leg; Paris edged it 5–4, a result that could have gone any way and very nearly went Bayern's.

The return was the calm after: 1–1, holding the lead, seeing it out. 6–5 on aggregate, the tightest two-leg margin since Monaco, and a place in the final.
