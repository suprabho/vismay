# Mapped: The Melodi Era

*From Bali in November 2022 to Rome in May 2026, Narendra Modi and Giorgia Meloni have met ten times in three and a half years. Behind the photo-ops sits the most concrete upgrade India has made to any G7 relationship in a decade: a Strategic Partnership, a ten-pillar Action Plan, two megadeals, an Indo-Mediterranean corridor, and a "Special Strategic Partnership" — the highest tier India grants any G7 capital except Paris, Tokyo and Washington.*

**By vizmaya · May 2026**

---

## 10

*Modi–Meloni interactions between November 2022 and May 2026 — eight in-person meetings and two formal phone calls*

That cadence is unusual for two countries with no formal alliance, no shared land border, and no security treaty. It is more frequent than Modi has met any other G7 leader except France's Macron, and more frequent than Meloni has met any non-EU leader at all.

Two of the ten were dedicated state visits — Meloni in Delhi in March 2023, Modi in Rome in May 2026. One was Modi as a G7 outreach guest at Borgo Egnazia. The remaining sit on G20, COP and phone-call sidelines. The architecture that came out of that calendar — Strategic Partnership in March 2023, Joint Strategic Action Plan in November 2024, Special Strategic Partnership in May 2026 — is the surviving residue of the pace.

---

## A relationship redrawn

The Indo-Italian file Meloni inherited in October 2022 was, by Indian standards, frozen. The AgustaWestland VVIP-helicopter bribery case had iced procurement decisions since 2013; the *Enrica Lexie* marines case had iced political ones since 2012. The 2020 PCA award and Rome's January 2022 dismissal of the marines charges had quietly closed both files — but no one in Delhi had yet picked up the receiver.

Meloni's first state visit to India in March 2023 reset the channel. Bilateral relations were elevated to a Strategic Partnership, with a four-year defence action plan, an ICCR Chair, and a Startup Bridge. Six months later at the New Delhi G20, Italy signed onto the India–Middle East–Europe Economic Corridor — and three months after that, walked out of China's Belt and Road. Neither legacy case has been politically mentioned in any Modi–Meloni statement since.

---

## Act I: Three architectures in three years

The Strategic Partnership of March 2023 was the architectural unlock. The Joint Strategic Action Plan signed at Rio in November 2024 turned it into a ten-pillar roadmap — political dialogue, trade, connectivity, science, space, energy, defence, security, migration, culture. The Special Strategic Partnership unveiled in Rome on 20 May 2026 added a Foreign-Minister-led annual review mechanism and the €20 billion trade target for 2029.

Plotted against the meeting calendar, the architecture lines up almost too neatly. Each tier was unveiled on Italian or Indian home soil; the multilateral sidelines were used to pre-negotiate. The three home-soil meetings carried the architectural breakthroughs. The seven sideline meetings and phone calls carried the implementation review.

---

## €14.25B

*Bilateral merchandise trade between India and Italy in calendar 2025 — nearly double the pandemic-year trough*

Trade has been the load-bearing wall of the upgrade. From €7.27 billion in 2020 to €14.25 billion in 2025, the line has bent almost monotonically upward; Italy is now India's fourth-largest trading partner in the EU, after Germany, Belgium, and the Netherlands.

The composition has shifted too. India runs a €2.85 billion goods surplus, exporting ready-made garments, leather, iron and steel, motor-vehicle components, gems and pharmaceuticals; Italy sends back textile and packaging machinery, machine tools, automotive components and luxury goods. Services trade reached €1.33 billion in 2025, with India's IT and telecom exports growing at close to twenty percent.

---

## Trade, nearly doubled

The 96 percent jump since 2020 puts Italy among the fastest-growing trading partners India has in the European Union. The Indian Embassy in Rome reports Italian exports to India growing 9.42 percent in calendar 2025 to €5.70 billion — the result, in part, of the Italian Trade Agency's "Strategic Support for the Indian Market" measure launched in September 2025.

The €20 billion target Meloni and Modi set on 20 May 2026 implies seven to eight percent compound growth from here. It is feasible. But it depends on three things outside the bilateral itself — the EU-India FTA closing, machine-tool tariff frictions being addressed, and pharma certification harmonising. None of those are in Italian hands alone.

---

## €3.8B

*Tata Motors' announced takeover of Iveco — the largest Indian acquisition of an Italian industrial group on record*

Announced on 30 July 2025; conditionally cleared by Italy's Golden Power review on 10 November 2025; closing expected in 2026. The combined group will produce around 540,000 commercial vehicles a year with India and Europe as twin home markets, and a combined turnover near €22 billion.

The deal architecture is itself a template. Iveco's defence-vehicle arm was carved out and sold separately to Leonardo for €1.7 billion — ringfencing sovereign-sensitive armoured-vehicle production from foreign ownership, while clearing the way for Indian capital in the civilian commercial-vehicle business. Future Indian acquisitions of Italian mid-caps will likely follow the same shape.

---

## Act II: Megadeals that anchor a decade

Indian foreign direct investment stock in Italy stood at €490 million at the end of 2024. Once Tata–Iveco closes, the figure jumps nearly tenfold — €4.3 billion of stock from a single transaction, before any other deal completes.

Two more files sit behind it. Leonardo and Adani Defence & Aerospace signed an MoU on 3 February 2026 to build an integrated helicopter manufacturing ecosystem in India around the AW169M and AW109 TrekkerM — Indian armed-forces demand is projected at over a thousand helicopters over the next decade. Fincantieri and Cochin Shipyard have an active shipbuilding MoU; Maire Tecnimont is running a US$255 million plastic-recycling project in Gujarat; Ferrovie dello Stato is upgrading the Delhi–Jaipur railway line for €207 million. The big number is Tata–Iveco. The picture is fuller than that.

---

## The defence file reopens

The defence-cooperation MoU Rajnath Singh signed in Rome in October 2023 was the first one in over a decade. What it unlocked is more visible at sea than on paper: ITS Cavour brought its carrier strike group, including its F-35Bs, to Goa in October 2024; ITS Amerigo Vespucci docked in Mumbai with the "Villaggio Italia" expo in late November; INS Tamal called at Naples in August 2025; INS Trikand at Taranto in September.

April 2026 added the formal scaffolding. Defence Minister Guido Crosetto's maiden visit to New Delhi exchanged a 2026–27 Military Cooperation Plan with Singh, alongside an MoU between the Society of Indian Defence Manufacturers and Italy's AIAD. The India–EU Security and Defence Partnership concluded in January 2026 provides additional canopy.

---

## Act III: India's Mediterranean landfall

The India–Middle East–Europe Economic Corridor that Italy signed at the New Delhi G20 in September 2023 runs from Mumbai and Mundra through Saudi Arabia and the UAE, across Israel, and into Europe via Italian, Greek and French ports. Italy is the de facto principal European endpoint — a function of Trieste's hinterland reach into Central Europe, Genoa's Mediterranean throughput, and the Mattei Plan for Africa that Meloni has tied explicitly to IMEC's southern flank.

Modi used his 20 May 2026 Rome press conference to call IMEC "wonderful potential for our trade"; Meloni framed Italy as a "logistical platform projected into the Mediterranean and Indo-Pacific." For Delhi the corridor depends on a credible EU terminal partner. For Rome it is the strategic complement to North Africa. Red Sea disruptions and Italy's Operation Aspides participation have, paradoxically, sharpened the case rather than weakened it.

---

## €20B

*The bilateral trade target Modi and Meloni set for 2029 in Rome on 20 May 2026*

Seven to eight percent compound growth from 2025. The target is the headline of the Special Strategic Partnership and the operational benchmark for everything below it — the JSAP, the 22nd Joint Commission for Economic Cooperation, the SIDM–AIAD industrial dialogue, the INNOVIT India hub.

Whether it is hit will depend on three things outside the bilateral itself: the EU-India FTA closing in 2026 or 2027; Italian SME participation in Indian public procurement; and the Mediterranean security picture that determines whether IMEC volumes materialise. Slippage below five percent compound growth in either 2026 or 2027 would force a renegotiated, lower target.

---

## 186,833

*Indian nationals registered as residents in Italy as of January 2025 — the largest Indian community in the European Union*

Per the Consulate General of India in Milan, citing ISTAT, the figure makes Italy the seventh-largest foreign-resident community in the country at 3.25 percent of all foreign nationals, the largest Indian diaspora in the European Union, and the second-largest in Europe after the United Kingdom. MEA briefs report a larger figure of around 250,000 once persons of Indian origin and the undocumented are included.

The number sits behind the Migration and Mobility Agreement signed in Rome on 2 November 2023, which reserved 12,000 non-seasonal and 12,000 seasonal Italian work-visa slots for Indian nationals across 2023 to 2025. Active recruitment lanes — healthcare, eldercare, dairy and agriculture — are the medium-term test of whether the quota turns into a renewed second-generation MMA for 2026 to 2030.

---

## A people-to-people wave

Direct air connectivity is now the densest it has been since the early 2000s. ITA Airways' Rome–Delhi service, relaunched in December 2022 on three weekly flights, runs daily on Airbus A330-900neo by late 2024. Air India's Delhi–Milan resumed in March 2023 and now operates twice daily; the Delhi–Rome route relaunched on 25 March 2026. Italian low-cost carrier Neos flies Bergamo–Amritsar weekly.

Tourism flows are catching up with the schedules. Italian arrivals to India rebounded past their 2019 baseline of around 213,000 in 2024 — one of just six source markets the Indian Ministry of Tourism flags as having fully recovered. Indian arrivals to Italy reached around 550,000 in 2023 per ENIT, with ten percent year-on-year growth into early 2025. The Executive Programme of Cultural Cooperation 2023–27, the ICCR Chair at the University of Milan, and Yoga Day at Castel Sant'Angelo are the institutional version of the same wave.

---

## A meme as a soft-power instrument

The "Melodi" hashtag began as a Meloni caption on a December 2023 COP28 selfie. It has been deliberately revived since: "Hello from the Melodi team" at Borgo Egnazia in June 2024; the Hindi line *Parishram hi safalta ki kunji hai* — "hard work is the key to success" — that Meloni opened her 20 May 2026 Rome press conference with; and Modi's bilateral-dinner gift of a packet of Indian "Melody" toffees that same evening.

The toffee beat — picked up by ANI and PTI, reshared by Modi on his official handles, viral within hours in both countries — is harder to dismiss than it looks. It has helped a centre-right Italian government be politically saleable in Indian liberal media, and a Hindu-nationalist Indian government be politically saleable in Italian centre-left media. Most G20 leader friendships fade after the photo-op cycle ends. This one has been kept on its feet by stagecraft as much as by the JSAP.

## Methodology & sources

Trade figures are drawn from India's Ministry of Commerce (Indian fiscal years, US dollars) and from the Indian Embassy in Rome's calendar-year Euro series; the two are not directly comparable and both are reported above where relevant. 2025 figures are provisional.

The meetings log, agreement timeline, defence milestones and naval port calls draw on the Embassy of India in Rome, the Italian Embassy in New Delhi, the Indian Ministry of External Affairs, the Italian governo.it press archive, ANI and PTI wires, and Decode39's bilateral reporting through May 2026.

The 186,833 figure is the ISTAT January 2025 number reported by the Consulate General of India in Milan. The MEA's larger figure of around 250,000 includes persons of Indian origin and the undocumented; the discrepancy is reported above. Tourism figures cite the Indian Ministry of Tourism's *India Tourism Data Compendium 2025* and ENIT releases for 2024 and the first half of 2025. The Banca d'Italia Survey on International Tourism is the most authoritative primary source for Indian arrivals.

*This is an editorial framing of a documented bilateral inflection. Figures and dates are reproduced as cited; the argument about what the cluster of architectures, megadeals and meetings adds up to is the author's.*
