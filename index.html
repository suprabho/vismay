DRC · Ituri Province

Where the cases sit.

Three health zones carry the outbreak: Rwampara with the largest contact list, Bunia as the provincial capital and transit hub, and Mongbwalu — a high-traffic gold-mining town that is the suspected origin.

All three sit inside the active operational zone of armed groups including ADF, CODECO and M23. WHO Director-General Tedros said publicly: "Contact tracing is nearly impossible while bombs are falling."

Conflict-driven gap

Roughly 621 contacts listed in Rwampara alone; only a small fraction were being actively traced as of late May.

Cumulative human cases since 1976

Five countries hold 95% of all Ebola history.

DRC has had more outbreaks than any other country (17, with the 2026 event), but they have stayed mostly small. The 2014–2016 West African epidemic alone accounts for more than half of all human Ebola cases ever recorded.

Five species. Three that matter for humans.

"Ebola" is not one disease — and 2026 is the species we've done the least work on.

The licensed vaccine Ervebo and the licensed monoclonal antibodies Inmazeb and Ebanga target Orthoebolavirus zairense. They are not expected to cross-protect against Bundibugyo. Conflating species is the single most common analytical error in Ebola reporting.

Why species matters in 2026

  • The Ervebo stockpile (>47,500 doses deployed in DRC's 2025 Kasai outbreak) cannot be relied on for BDBV.
  • Oxford/Serum Institute of India have a ChAdOx1-BDBV candidate; targeted ~2–3 months to trial-readiness from May 2026.
  • Monoclonal antibody MBP134 and remdesivir are under consideration for randomized trials.
  • WHO calls evidence on Ervebo cross-protection "limited and inconclusive."

Phylogenetics · Nextstrain BDBV build

A new spillover, not a survivor relapse.

Three new 2026 genomes — sequenced by Institut National de Recherche Biomédicale (DRC) and Uganda Virus Research Institute — have been placed against 34 prior BDBV genomes on Nextstrain.

The 2026 viruses cluster tightly with each other but are genetically distinct from both the 2007 Uganda and 2012 DRC lineages. The simplest reading: a fresh zoonotic spillover from an animal reservoir (probably a fruit bat), not human-survivor persistence.

What would change the picture

Five tripwires worth watching.

Case outside the corridor

A confirmed case anywhere outside DRC–Uganda–South Sudan.

Local Uganda transmission

Sustained chains in Kampala vs imported cases only.

ChAdOx1-BDBV trial starts

Oxford / Serum Institute of India candidate moves to field.

Ervebo cross-protection

Evidence — currently absent — that Ervebo helps against BDBV.

A second large-city cluster

Goma is the most cited concern. Spillover to a second regional hub would mark a phase change.

For builders

The authoritative data is human-readable. The machine-readable data is community-built.

There is no official, real-time, programmatic case/death API for the 2026 outbreak. WHO DON / AFRO / Africa CDC publish PDFs and HTML. The cleanest CSVs come from third-party academic and humanitarian repositories, and they are mostly historical.

Caveat for trackers

Numbers move daily. Re-classification of suspected → confirmed produces downward revisions that look like errors if not handled explicitly. Cross-check WHO + CDC + ECDC at every refresh.
